FAQ

How Do Ad Blockers Work?

Ad blockers operate by examining the HTML of a webpage as it loads, checking for elements that match a list of known ad-related patterns. When a match is found, the ad blocker creates a rule to block that specific ad element from being displayed. This prevents banners, pop-ups, and sponsored content from appearing on the page, resulting in a cleaner browsing experience.

Do Ad Blockers Make Websites Run Slower?

Typically, ad blockers can actually speed up website loading times because they stop the browser from downloading and displaying ads and other unnecessary elements. However, some websites may detect the use of an ad blocker and either refuse to load or prompt users to disable it, which can slow down the browsing experience. Additionally, certain ad blockers may use additional resources, potentially causing a slight decrease in browsing speed.

Can Ad Blockers See Your Passwords?

No, trustworthy ad blockers do not have access to your passwords. They are specifically designed to block ads and unwanted content without intercepting sensitive information. However, it is essential to download ad blockers from reputable sources to avoid malicious software disguised as ad blockers, which could potentially steal private data.

What Are Online Trackers?

Online trackers are scripts embedded in websites that collect data about your browsing activities and send this information to third-party companies. This data is often used for targeted advertising and can compromise your privacy. Ad blockers can help protect your data by preventing these trackers from collecting and transmitting information about your online behavior.
